**2. CAGR Trends**

**Limitation:**
The provided IQVIA dataset offers a snapshot of 'Total Sales' and market share for various therapies across different regions at a single point in time. To calculate Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) and identify sales growth or decline patterns, multi-year sales data is required. The current data does not include historical sales figures (e.g., sales from previous years), which are necessary to determine the rate of change over time.

**Conclusion:**
Therefore, based on the extracted data, it is not possible to calculate CAGR values per therapy, identify sales growth/decline patterns per therapy and region, or highlight the top growing therapies.

**3. Competitive Landscape (Therapy-wise)**

In the absence of company-specific data, the competitive landscape is analyzed through the lens of therapy areas vying for market share and resources.

*   **Dominant Therapies:** Oncology and Cardiology are the clear market leaders, collectively capturing over 70% of the total sales. This indicates a highly concentrated market where these two areas are the primary "competitors" for investment and focus.
*   **Mid-Tier Therapy:** Neurology holds a significant, albeit smaller, share, suggesting it is a relevant but not dominant player. Its sales are well-distributed across USA, France, and Spain.
*   **Niche/Developing Therapy:** Dermatology represents the smallest segment, indicating either a niche market or an area with significant untapped potential, especially given its zero sales in most European countries listed.
*   **Geographical Strengths:** The USA is a critical market for all major therapy areas, acting as a central hub for sales. Germany is a strong secondary market, particularly for the dominant Oncology and Cardiology segments. France shows strength in Oncology and Neurology, while Italy, Spain, and the UK have more focused contributions.
